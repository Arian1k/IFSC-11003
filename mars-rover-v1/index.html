Introduction of The Red Planet

NASA first started exploring Mars in 1964. Making their goals to try to study, explore, understand, and analyze this planet. Since then, they have done many studies and sent out many spacecrafts to help us answer many questions including one main question, "Is there another planet habitable to humans'?"

It all started with the Mariner 4 Mission, sending a spacecraft to just to fly by the planet, so that they could get an idea of what this planet looked like up close. Since then, we have developed our technology, so instead of just spacecrafts to just get a look. We started sending rovers to actually land on this planet to explore.

Their exploration and curiosity has tremendously helped mankind! Now use this page to explore your curiosity. Learn about what was put into each mission and what the outcomes were to those missions. Find out how these missions are shaping our future and our future explorers, which could be 'YOU'.

Spirit

    Spirit was launched on June 10, 2003 to go explore Mars. The mission for Spirit was to see what was there besides water. Spirit was a very successful rover, it uncovered that Mars is much wetter than anticipated. It also captured a picture of bedrock that showed Mars's geological history. It also captured current time moments of dust devils(a whirlwind of dirt caused by wind).
    Unfortunately, Spirits mission was discontinued on May 25, 2011 due to the fact that Spirit got stuck in mud on Mars and eventually lost contact to Nasa. Spirit had an incredible run of informing Nasa for a little over 6 years. According to Nasa, it lasted 25 times longer than what they anticipated it to last. Fun fact as well, Spirit is the twin rover to 'Opportunity.'
